Role Overview For Senior Product Manager (Acquisition & Monetisation)
As Senior Product Manager, you’ll own the product lifecycle end-to-end—from vision to execution. You’ll
work with leadership to define the roadmap, drive data-backed decisions, and deliver impactful
experiences for millions of users. This is a high-ownership role, requiring strategic thinking, strong
execution, and deep empathy for users.
Key Responsibilities
Product Vision & Strategy: Define and own product roadmap for your charter aligned with
company goals.
Data-Driven Decision Making: Leverage analytics, user research, and market insights to identify
problems, opportunities and improve product KPIs.
End-to-End Ownership: Lead the product lifecycle - from concept, requirements gathering, and
prioritisation to launch and post-launch optimisation.
User-Centric Design: Partner with UX/UI teams to deliver intuitive and delightful user experience.
Cross-Functional Leadership: Collaborate with engineering, design, marketing, and operations to
execute product initiatives with confidence.
Performance Optimisation: Monitor KPIs, and iterate to drive conversion, retention, and revenue.
Market Awareness: Stay ahead of trends, competitors, and emerging technologies to maintain
product leadership.
What You Bring
Education: Engineering or Management degree from a premier institute — IIT, BITS Pilani, IIM,
ISB preferred.
Experience: Must have minimum 4+ years of core B2C product management experience, with
measurable impact on Revenue, ARPU, Conversion Rate, Repeat Transactions, Retention,
and Engagement.
Past KPIs must include Revenue, Conversion, ARPU, and Repeat Transactions.
Proven track record of delivering impactful digital products in high-growth environments.
Strong analytical skills with expertise in interpreting complex datasets.
Excellent communication and stakeholder management skills.
Bonus Points
Past Founding or Entrepreneurship experience
Nice to have: Experience managing products on both App and Web platforms.
Experience in e-commerce or supply chain product ecosystems.
